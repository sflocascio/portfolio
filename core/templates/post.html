The Little Triangle

A web app built to offer parents in North Carolina’s Triangle Region a platform to discover, review, and gain valuable insights about the best activities and offerings in their area. Visit at thelittletriangle.com
{% comment %}
{% endcomment %}

The Problem

Three questions nearly all parents ask themselves when they are about to register a child for a brand new class or program:

Is it safe? Is it worth the money? Is it worth the time?

The concept for The Little Triangle was envisioned by such a parent looking for a better way to get answers to these questions. Our solution was to build a place where parents could discover quality events and activities in the Triangle, while empowering them with a platform to share valuable insights and feedback with each other.

Challenges and Opportunities

Similar offerings lacked a clutter free, colorful, and engaging user experience. Our site design embraced a minimal and colorful design which allowed us the opportunity to bring in featured content relevant to our users.


Cards for the win - Images and icons on detail cards give users a snapshot of events and businesses before clicking


Reviews and Ratings - A key feature of the site, rankings and reviews gave our users a way to share valuable insights about activities and experiences

Design Theory

Two distinct branding fonts were chosen to deliver a clear message within the site while still allowing it to maintain its youthful roots. From the beginning, I wanted the website be be bright and colorful, reflecting the content inside it. Inspired by the aesthetic of crayola crayons, I chose a color palette that incorporated primary colors. The majority of colors used in the site are the actual color codes from Crayolas original eight pack. Icons deliver a consistent voice throughout the site and add a much needed visual element that we found was lacking from other sites.
