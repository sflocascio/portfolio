The Oak House

A web app built to offer parents in North Carolina’s Triangle Region a platform to discover, review, and gain valuable insights about the best activities, camps, and offerings in their area.



The Problem

Three questions nearly all parents ask themselves when they are about to register a child for a brand new class or program:

Is it safe? Is it worth the money? Is it worth the time?

The concept for the little Triangle was envisioned by such a parent, looking for a place to discover quality events and activities for her child, while getting valuable insights and feedback from other parents in her area

{% comment %}

Discovery

Polling - After reaching out to popular parenting forums and groups in the region we received overwhelming positive feedback, confirming a need for the concept my team envisioned


Competitive Analysis - A quick comparison of current offerings in our area helped us define opportunities and features that our concept would capitalize on

{% endcomment %} {% comment %}

Design Theory

Two distinct branding fonts were chosen to deliver a clear message within the site while allowing it to maintain its youthful roots. From the beginning I wanted the website be be bright and colorful, reflecting the content inside it. Inspired by the aesthetic of crayola crayons, I chose a color palette that incorporated primary colors, many of which were the color codes from Crayolas original eight pack. Icons deliver a consistent voice throughout the site and add a much needed visual element that we found was lacking from other sites
