About

{% endcomment %}

I’m a natural creative who loves problem solving and the journey of building new things. My first exposure to coding happened sometime in the Myspace era when I began adding HTML and CSS to make my bands (and other bands) pages look better. I thought that was really cool. It was this investigative process that led me to be intrigued with coding and fascinated by the things that can be accomplished with technology.

I’m quite fond of the outdoors, recording music, fishing, ping pong, and (recently) cooking . I love to let my passions inspire my work whenever possible and have always loved work where I’ve able to help others. I recently received my Full stack Certification from Momentum Learning in Durham, NC and am currently working freelance on projects set to release in 2019.

Feel free to contact me if you want to discuss a project or opportunity.

UI / UX

{% endcomment %}

“Something that looks great but is difficult to use is exemplary of great UI and poor UX. While Something very usable that looks terrible is exemplary of great UX and poor UI.”

This is one of my favorite expressions of UI and UX. My goal and approach with UI and UX is to connect business goals with user needs through a process of testing and refinement that leads to a product that satisfies both sides of the relationship.

In other words, to make something that looks great AND is very usable.
